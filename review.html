My Mother's Atlas

By Leela Thomas · Narrative poetry · 3 min read · Published 2 days ago
She kept the map in a steel trunk alongside the certificates, the photographs, the folded letter that was never sent. Every crease held a country she had left in portions.
She did not grieve aloud. Grief, for her, was a domestic thing — folded into the same drawer as the ration cards, useful only when you knew where to look.
I am learning to read her silences the way cartographers read coastlines — every indentation a word she chose not to say, every peninsula a memory still arriving.
